Ratings Overview

Batter Rating

Overall hitter matchup score. It blends recent batter form, probable pitcher vulnerability, park factors, and conservative batter-vs-pitcher history.

HR Rating

Power-specific score. It focuses on batter power, barrels, hard contact, season HR pace, pitcher HR/contact risk, HR park factor, weather, and BvP HR history.

Pitcher Quality

Probable starter run/contact suppression. Higher means the pitcher has limited strong contact and hits well, adjusted with hits allowed per inning.

Pitcher K Rating

Strikeout-specific starter score for K prop context. It blends season/last-five K averages, K per inning, and typical starter workload.

Bullpen Rating

Team bullpen support score. It uses recent reliever contact allowed plus estimated recent workload, so tired or shaky bullpens grade lower.

Weather Rating

Simple run/HR environment score from game-time temperature and wind speed. It helps HR Rating and lightly informs Model Pick.

Market

Sportsbook/run-line side. This comes from the betting market and is separate from our model.

Pick

Our first-pass game pick. It weighs team hitter matchup strength, starter strength, bullpen quality/rest, weather, and home field.

Total Lean

Projected total runs compared with the market O/U. Over or Under only appears when the model is at least 0.5 runs away from the market.

Sort Modes

Batter Rating sorts for overall hitter matchup quality. HR Rating sorts for power and home-run profile.

Lineup Status

A = active/confirmed in lineup. NA = not active/not in lineup. TBD = lineup unavailable or not posted yet.

These are transparent ranking scores, not guaranteed probabilities. The goal is to surface strong spots quickly, then let you compare hitter quality, game context, and market information together.
